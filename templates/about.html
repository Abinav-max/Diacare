About DiaCare

DiaCare is a supportive health screening tool designed to predict your future risk of developing Gestational Diabetes and Type 2 Diabetes.

Unlike traditional tests that only measure your current blood sugar level, DiaCare uses a smart predictive model to look ahead. By checking simple demographic and vital metrics early, it helps you spot potential future risks, raise wellness awareness, and take proactive steps toward healthy living.

Diabetes Mellitus & Pregnancy

Diabetes Mellitus is a group of metabolic diseases characterized by persistent hyperglycemia (high blood sugar levels) resulting from defects in insulin secretion, insulin action, or both.

1. Type-2 Diabetes: Characterized by insulin resistance, where body cells fail to respond appropriately to insulin. Over time, the pancreas cannot produce enough insulin to maintain homeostasis. It is strongly linked to genetics, physical inactivity, and obesity.

2. Gestational Diabetes (GDM): Occurs during pregnancy when placenta-produced hormones block the action of maternal insulin (insulin resistance). Without sufficient compensation, blood sugar rises. Left untreated, GDM can cause macrosomia (large birth weight), neonatal hypoglycemia, pre-eclampsia, and elevates the risk of permanent Type-2 diabetes progression for the mother later in life.

Clinical Parameters Explained

DiaCare utilizes several clinical metrics to evaluate diabetic risk index:

  • Plasma Glucose (2h OGTT)

    Plasma glucose levels measured 2 hours after drinking a 75g oral glucose solution. This is a gold standard for detecting glucose intolerance and gestational stress.

  • Body Mass Index (BMI)

    An index of body fat based on weight and height (kg/m²). Higher BMI is a primary physical marker for systemic insulin resistance.

  • Serum Insulin

    Measures the concentration of active insulin in the bloodstream post-loading, providing vital indicators of pancreatic beta-cell health and compensation efficiency.
